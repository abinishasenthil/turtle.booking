Threat, consequences and Solutions

Plastic Pollution

Plastic pollution is a major threat to sea turtles, who often mistake plastic debris for food. This can cause blockages in their digestive system and lead to starvation or other health issues. Plastic pollution can also entangle turtles, causing them to drown or suffer injuries.

One way to prevent plastic pollution from harming sea turtles is to reduce our use of single-use plastics, such as straws, bags, and bottles. We can also properly dispose of our trash and participate in beach cleanups to remove plastic debris from the environment.

Coastal Development

Coastal development, such as the construction of hotels, resorts, and marinas, can destroy important sea turtle nesting and feeding habitats. This can lead to a decline in turtle populations and a loss of biodiversity in marine ecosystems.

We can prevent the negative impacts of coastal development on sea turtles by supporting sustainable tourism practices and responsible coastal management. This can involve promoting eco-tourism, protecting important turtle habitats, and implementing regulations to limit the negative impacts of coastal development.

Learn more about how to prevent coastal development from harming sea turtles

Bycatch

Bycatch is a term used to describe the unintended capture of sea turtles (and other marine animals) in fishing gear. This can lead to injury or death for turtles, and can also contribute to population declines.

We can prevent sea turtle bycatch by implementing turtle-friendly fishing gear and practices, such as using circle hooks and nets with escape openings. Bycatch reduction devices can also be installed on fishing gear to minimize the number of sea turtles that are unintentionally caught.
