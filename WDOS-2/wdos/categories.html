Categories and Description of Turtles

Sri Lanka is home to a number of different sea turtle species, including the Olive Ridley, Green, Loggerhead, Hawksbill, and Leatherback turtles. Because of habitat loss, pollution, and other threats, all species are regarded as critically endangered or endangered, each with distinctive traits and behaviors of their own. Sea turtle nesting places are protected, turtle population data is gathered, and public awareness campaigns are conducted in Sri Lanka as part of conservation efforts. We can ensure that these amazing creatures survive for many generations by cooperating to protect them.

The Categories

Turtle Category Image Description
Green Turtle The green turtle is the largest of the hard-shelled sea turtles, weighing up to 700 pounds. It is found in tropical and subtropical waters worldwide, including the coasts of Sri Lanka. Green turtles are herbivores and feed mainly on seagrass and algae.
Hawksbill Turtle The hawksbill turtle is a medium-sized sea turtle that is found in warm waters around the world. It has a distinctive beak-like mouth and is named for its narrow, pointed head. Hawksbill turtles feed mainly on sponges, which makes them important for maintaining the health of coral reef ecosystems.
Olive Ridley Turtle These turtles are found in warm waters of the Pacific, Atlantic, and Indian Oceans. They are named after their olive-colored carapace, which is heart-shaped and rounded. Olive Ridley turtles are known for their mass nesting events, called arribadas, where thousands of females come ashore to lay their eggs at the same time. This is a fascinating phenomenon to witness!
